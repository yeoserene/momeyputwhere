Be calculative.

Creator: Serene Yeo (v0.1)

This calculator app is meant to help several groups of people -- those who just began their savings journey, those who are too lazy or find it too troublesome to do comparisons, those who need cash assets liquidity and of course, many others who may possibly find this app useful in one way or another.

Its purpose is to help you easily and quickly visualize which is the best allocation of money across the savings accounts in Singapore that has one of the highest interest rates, namely DBS Multiplier, OCBC 360 and UOB One based on the criteria that you can fulfill.

Unfortunately, things don't come free. These banks often require you to fulfill some stated criteria, so be sure to check them out properly.

Once again, this tool is nowhere near perfect, as it makes its fair share of assumptions.

With no further matters, ENJOY!


How best to use this tool?:
I would suggest you to key the entries in, get a good overall feeling about the interest rates you are likely to get, and visit the desired bank's website! Links provided.

Some personal experiences:
Sit down and think through what you are able to fulfill. In summary, this is my take on the savings accounts:
DBS Multiplier : As long as you can credit your salary and transact in 1 or more other categories (CC spending/ loan/ wealth) with a minimum of $2000, interest rates start varying from 1.55%-3.50%.
OCBC 360 : As long as you have a minimum of $2000 salary credit, can spend $500 or more on your CC, and can increase your account balance by at least $500 every month, interest rates start varying from 1.8% on the first $35k to 2.7% on the next $35k.
UOB One : As long as you can hit the minimum $500 credit spending EVERY month, with salary credit or 3 GIRO arrangements, interest rates start from 1.85% on the first $15k to 3.88% on the $60k-$75k range!

If you have no problems hitting ALL the criteria and have savings of up to $75k, the effective interest rate at OCBC is 2.25% on $70k. (wealth excluded from computation as it is only valid for first 12 months), at UOB is 2.436% on $75k. At DBS, it depends on how much your eligible transactions are per month. If your total eligible transactions amount to $5k-15k with two more categories fulfilled, interest rate stands at 2.20% on your first $50k.
